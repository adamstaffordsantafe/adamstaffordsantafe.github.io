Goals

Stuff I'm looking for
in a new job.

Innovation

    I believe innovation is a process that takes hard work, dedication, and critical thinking. The process is critical to creating outstanding value.

     

    Innovation can also feel threatening, and a work environment must embrace change and personal initiative in order to realize and develop truly innovative products, services
    and experiences.

Strong Culture

    Culture cannot be faked. All of the ping-ping tables and kegerators in the world cannot make a company’s culture vibrant or cooperative.

     

    The inner soul of any organization tends to be ridgid, and tends to be set fairly early on. This inner spirit needs to be reinforced with clearly expressed values and vision to ensure cohesion among team members.

Obsessable Projects

    Some of my greatest ideas have come to me in the shower. I thrive in an environment where I am immersed in a project to the point where I am literally waking up thinking about it every morning.

     

    I find that a personal sense of ownership is critical to creating truly obsessable projects.

Hard Working Team of Builders

    Work ethic cannot be faked. By this I don’t mean showing up at 7am and leaving at 7pm Monday-Saturday. I mean doing what it takes to be exceptional for your team, often at the cost of personal pleasures or pursuits. I like to have fun and throw dinner parties, but work comes first.

     

    As far as people go, I like to build stuff, and I like to work with other people who like to build stuff.

Fun & Creativity

    Fun is really important. Work is fun when you’re doing the right work. Work is also hard and challenging, but good work is definitely fun.

     

    Creativity is awesome and exhausting. Building stuff is always an act of creativity (unless its furntiture assembly by numbers). Let’s get creative!

Data Driven Decision Making

    Data! I love data! Making decisions based on information is so much more effective than basing decisions on gut feel.

     

    That doesn’t mean you don’t need good instincts; instead, a healthy dose of solid datapoints mixed with creative problem solving practices tends to lead to the best results.
